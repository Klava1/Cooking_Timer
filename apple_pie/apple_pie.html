Classic Charlotte Cake

An important sweet dish in Soviet and post-Soviet history. Light, fluffy dough with a maximum of apple filling — Charlotte has always had the image of a pleasant, simple, yet tasty and dietary dish. We strongly recommend using sour apple varieties, like Antonovka. They can be used either raw or slightly caramelized in a pan beforehand. And don't skimp on the sugar. It magically distributes in the right amount in the dough, and the excess forms a sweet crust.

Ingredients

  • Sugar - 1 cup
  • Chicken Egg - 5 pieces
  • Wheat Flour - 1 cup
  • Apple - 7 pieces
  • Vegetable Oil - 1 tablespoon
  • Baking Powder - ½ teaspoon

Cooking Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven. Separate the egg whites from the yolks. Whip the whites into a firm foam with a pinch of salt, gradually adding sugar.
  2. Continue whipping, adding the yolks one at a time, then the baking powder and flour. The dough should have a consistency similar to sour cream.
  3. Grease a baking sheet with vegetable oil. Pour half of the dough onto the sheet, evenly distribute the apple slices cut into wedges, and cover with the second half of the dough.
  4. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven. Keep at 200 degrees Celsius for 3 minutes, then reduce to 180 degrees and bake for 20–25 minutes.
